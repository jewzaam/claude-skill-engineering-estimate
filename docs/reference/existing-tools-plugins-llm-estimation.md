# Existing Tools/Plugins for LLM-Assisted Estimation

> Research Date: 2026-03-20
> Generated By: Claude Code (claude-opus-4-6)

## TL;DR

The landscape of AI/LLM-assisted estimation tools spans five categories: (1) dedicated AI estimation platforms for software projects, (2) Jira/PM tool plugins that use ML for story point prediction, (3) AI sprint planning assistants, (4) developer productivity measurement platforms that track AI impact on estimates, and (5) academic research prototypes. No tool currently adjusts estimates specifically for AI coding assistant usage. The gap between "AI that estimates work" and "estimation that accounts for AI doing the work" remains largely unfilled.

## 4. LLM-Based Estimation Research (Using GPT/Claude Directly)

### 4.1 Anthropic's Estimation Accuracy Research

- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/research/estimating-productivity-gains
- **Publication:** November 2025
- **Full paper:** https://assets.anthropic.com/m/28fda2ad148e2bf5/original/Estimating-AI-productivity-gains-from-Claude-conversations.pdf
- **Methodology:** Tested Claude's time estimation capabilities against a dataset of thousands of real-world software development tasks gathered from JIRA tickets for open-source repositories, with both developer estimates and actual tracked completion times.
- **Key constraint:** Claude received only the title and description of JIRA tickets, while human developers had full context on the codebase.
- **Results (on a subset of 1,000 tasks):**
  - Human developers: Spearman's rho = 0.50 correlation with actual times; Pearson r_log = 0.67 on log values
  - Claude's estimates: Spearman's rho = 0.44 (similar directional correlation to developers)
  - Claude "significantly overestimates short tasks and underestimates long ones"
- **Broader findings:** Analysis of 100,000 real AI conversations. Software developers contribute most (19%) to total labor productivity gain attributable to AI. Current AI technology cutting task times by up to 80% in some cases.
- **Limitations acknowledged:** Cannot account for additional time humans spend validating AI output outside conversations.
- **Source:** [Anthropic Research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/estimating-productivity-gains)

## 5. Developer Productivity Measurement Platforms

These tools do not estimate work upfront but measure the impact of AI tools on actual delivery, which is critical for calibrating future estimates.

### 5.1 DX AI Measurement Framework

- **URL:** https://getdx.com/research/measuring-ai-code-assistants-and-agents/
- **Authors:** Abi Noda (co-founder/CEO) and Laura Tacho (CTO) of DX
- **Partners:** Developed with GitHub, Dropbox, Atlassian, Booking.com
- **What it does:** Research-based framework for measuring AI coding tool impact across three dimensions: utilization, impact, and cost.
- **Key metrics:**
  - Direct: AI-driven time savings per developer per week
  - Indirect: PR throughput, Perceived Rate of Delivery, Developer Experience Index
  - Cost: Per-use cost optimization, ROI identification
- **AI code detection:** File system-level observability that detects the difference between human typing and AI-generated batch modifications by analyzing rate and pattern of file changes in real-time. Works across all IDEs and AI tools.
- **Key finding:** "Even leading organizations are only reaching around 60% active usage of AI tools."
- **Key quote:** "Headlines promise '2x productivity improvements' and '30% of code written by AI,' but these claims rarely match what organizations experience on the ground."
- **Source:** [DX Research](https://getdx.com/research/measuring-ai-code-assistants-and-agents/), [DX AI Measurement Framework](https://getdx.com/blog/introducing-the-ai-measurement-framework/)

### 5.2 Faros AI

- **URL:** https://www.faros.ai/
- **What it does:** Connects Claude Code usage to business outcomes. Tracks adoption patterns, calculates cost per engineering output, reveals where AI accelerates vs. creates bottlenecks.
- **Key findings from their analysis:**
  - "21% more tasks completed, 98% more PRs merged" with AI
  - BUT: DORA metrics (deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, time to restore) were unchanged
  - "Code review times increased 91%"
  - "Code generation got faster but everything downstream of it didn't"
- **The Productivity Paradox:** "When one stage of the pipeline accelerates and the rest stays flat, you get a pile-up at the next bottleneck, not faster delivery."
- **Timeline guidance:** Expect 2-4 weeks productivity drop during learning, 2-3 months before patterns stabilize, 6 months before confident ROI assessment.
- **Source:** [Faros AI Blog](https://www.faros.ai/blog/how-to-measure-claude-code-roi-developer-productivity-insights-with-faros-ai)

### 5.3 LinearB

- **URL:** https://linearb.io/
- **What it does:** Engineering productivity platform with AI measurement framework. PR labeling to track AI-generated code vs. human code.
- **Framework model:** Adoption -> Impact -> ROI
- **Key metrics:** Throughput (value delivery speed), Quality (bugs, tech debt, rework), Impact (AI vs. non-AI comparison).
- **Benchmarks:** Based on 8.1+ million PRs from 4,800 engineering teams across 42 countries.
- **Key insight:** "Executives are asking for outcomes. Boards are tracking AI productivity as a KPI. Engineering leaders are under pressure to demonstrate the ROI of generative AI."
- **Source:** [LinearB AI Measurement](https://linearb.io/blog/ai-measurement-framework)

### 5.4 TechEmpower AI Coding Tools Metrics

- **URL:** https://www.techempower.com/blog/2025/12/01/ai-coding-tools-metrics/
- **Published:** December 2025
- **Key categories tracked:** Utilization (DAU/WAU, % AI-generated code, % AI-assisted PRs), Throughput (PRs, tickets, story points, cycle time with/without AI), Quality (commit acceptance rates, rework rates, incident/defect trends for AI-touched vs. non-AI work).

## 7. Academic Research & Prototypes

### 7.1 Systematic Mapping Study: LLMs for Early-Stage Estimation (MDPI, Dec 2025)

- **Title:** "Large Language Models for Early-Stage Software Project Estimation: A Systematic Mapping Study"
- **Published in:** Applied Sciences, December 2025
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/15/24/13099
- **Scope:** 30 primary studies identified and categorized examining estimation targets, models used, reference/supportive techniques, and evaluation measures.
- **Key finding:** "Although several studies have investigated the use of pre-trained language models, such as BERT and GPT-2, for story point estimation, the research landscape remains fragmented."
- **Publication distribution:** Highest number published in 2024 (16 papers), followed by 2025 (8 papers).

### 7.2 GPT2SP: Transformer-Based Story Point Estimation (IEEE TSE, foundational)

- **Title:** "GPT2SP: A Transformer-Based Agile Story Point Estimation Approach"
- **Authors:** Fu & Tantithamthavorn
- **Published in:** IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9732669/
- **What it does:** Employs a GPT-2 pre-trained language model to capture word relationships with context and position awareness. Transferable to other projects while being interpretable.
- **Status:** Considered the state-of-the-art baseline in the field. Referenced by nearly all subsequent papers.
- **Limitation:** Shows higher error for cross-project experiments.

### 7.3 Search-Based Optimisation of LLM Learning Shots (Springer/arXiv, 2024)

- **Title:** "Search-Based Optimisation of LLM Learning Shots for Story Point Estimation"
- **Authors:** Tawosi, Alamir, & Liu
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-48796-5_9, https://arxiv.org/html/2403.08430v1
- **What it does:** Uses GPT-4 API (test-gpt4 engine, temperature=0.0) with search-based software engineering (SBSE) techniques to optimize few-shot learning examples for story point estimation.
- **Key contribution:** Demonstrated that optimizing which examples to provide the LLM significantly improves estimation effectiveness.

### 7.4 SBERT + Gradient Boosted Trees (MDPI, Aug 2024)

- **Title:** "Enhancing Agile Story Point Estimation: Integrating Deep Learning, Machine Learning, and NLP with SBERT and Gradient Boosted Trees"
- **Published in:** Applied Sciences, August 2024
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/14/16/7305
- **Results:** MAE of 2.15, MdAE of 1.85 -- representing a 12% improvement over baseline model.

### 7.5 Comparative Learning for Story Point Estimation (arXiv, 2025)

- **Title:** "Efficient Story Point Estimation With Comparative Learning"
- **Author:** Monoshiz Mahbub Khan et al.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.14642
- **Approach:** Instead of absolute story point assignment, developers compare pairs of items to indicate which requires more effort. ML model trained from comparative judgments. Lower cognitive burden on humans.
- **Key finding:** "State-of-the-art models like GPT2SP and FastText-SVM only make accurate predictions when trained on data from the same project."

### 7.6 ML-Based Estimation in Industry (IEEE, 2021)

- **Title:** "Machine Learning-based Estimation of Story Points in Agile Development: Industrial Experience and Lessons Learned"
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9582288/
- **Key finding:** "AI-based story point estimation with explanations is perceived as more useful and trustworthy than without explanations, highlighting the need for Explainable AI in this domain."

### 7.7 Cursor Impact Study (arXiv, 2025)

- **Title:** "Does AI-Assisted Coding Deliver? A Difference-in-Differences Study of Cursor's Impact on Software Projects"
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2511.04427v2
- **Key finding:** Adoption of Cursor results in a 1.84x baseline increase in lines added post-adoption. However, this leads to "a significant, large, but transient increase in project-level development velocity, along with a significant and persistent increase in static analysis warnings and code complexity."

### 7.8 ChatGPT Impact on Software Development (arXiv, 2024)

- **Title:** "Impact of the Availability of ChatGPT on Software Development: A Synthetic Difference in Differences Estimation using GitHub Data"
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11046
- **Methodology:** Uses government bans on ChatGPT as natural experiments with DID, Synthetic Control, and SDID methods.
- **Finding:** Significant positive impact on git pushes, repositories, and unique developers, particularly for high-level, general purpose, and shell scripting languages.

## 9. Identified Gap: Estimation Adjusting for AI Coding Assistants

### 9.1 The Missing Tool Category

No tool discovered in this research adjusts development estimates based on expected AI coding assistant usage. The two concepts -- "AI that estimates work" and "estimates that account for AI doing the work" -- remain separate and disconnected.

### 9.2 Emerging Methodology (Not Yet Tooled)

Several methodological proposals exist but have not been productized:

**Modified COCOMO with AI Augmentation Factor (AAF):**
- A 2023 study proposed a COCOMO extension incorporating an "AI augmentation factor" (AAF) ranging from 0.5 to 1.5, where lower values reflect higher AI productivity.
- Not yet standardized or implemented in any tool.
- Source: [Medium article](https://toashishagarwal.medium.com/new-rules-for-estimating-software-development-time-in-ai-era-460ec5347e1a)

**Task Segmentation Approach:**
- Break projects into AI-suitable and non-AI-suitable tasks, estimating each separately.
- Source: Multiple industry discussions.

**Observed Productivity Ranges:**
- McKinsey (2023): 20-50% reduction for specific tasks
- Industry consensus: 15-25% savings after prompt patterns stabilize
- Greenfield vs. brownfield: "The productivity boost in brownfield scenarios is much smaller -- some engineers estimate only a 10-30% speed-up at best."
- Source: Various industry reports

**The Perception Gap (Critical Caveat):**
- "Developers expected AI would speed them up by ~24%, and even after the experiment, they believed they had been faster by ~20% when using AI. In reality, the screen recordings and time logs told a different story -- a significant slowdown."
- Source: [Addyo Substack](https://addyo.substack.com/p/the-reality-of-ai-assisted-software)

**New Parameter: AI Tool Proficiency:**
- Team familiarity with AI tools and ability to craft effective prompts significantly affects productivity.
- No standardized way to measure or incorporate into estimates.

### 9.3 Measurement Platforms as Calibration Data

The productivity measurement platforms (DX, Faros, LinearB, TechEmpower) provide the data needed to calibrate estimation adjustments for AI coding assistant usage, but none of them currently feed back into estimation tools. This is a clear integration opportunity.

### 9.4 Key Gartner Statistic

"Organizations implementing AI-assisted agile tools report up to 40% faster release cycles and a 35% reduction in planning overhead."
- Source: Referenced in Zenhub blog post; original Gartner report not independently verified.

## Data Quality Caveats

1. **Accuracy claims from tool vendors** (CostGPT 80%, Devtimate 85-95%, Baseliner 95%+) are self-reported and not independently validated.
2. **Deloitte and Forrester claims** cited by Baseliner could not be traced to original published reports.
3. **Gartner statistics** cited by Zenhub could not be independently verified against original reports.
4. **User testimonials** on tool websites are curated and may not represent typical experiences.
5. **Academic accuracy metrics** (MAE, MdAE, Spearman correlations) are validated within their study conditions but may not generalize.
6. **Anthropic's research** is the most methodologically rigorous estimation study found but was conducted by the AI vendor itself.
7. **The Valletta Software comparison** is a single blog post test, not a peer-reviewed study. Sample size and methodology details are limited.
8. **EstimateAI** (estimate-ai.com) being listed for sale on Flippa suggests uncertain ongoing viability.
9. **WebFetch was denied** for anthropic.com, zenhub.com, and marketplace.atlassian.com pages, so data from those sources relies on search result summaries rather than direct page extraction.
